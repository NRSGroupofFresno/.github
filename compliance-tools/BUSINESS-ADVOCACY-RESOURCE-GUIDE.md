## What is a Non-Attorney Advocate?

A non-attorney advocate helps individuals and businesses by:

- Providing **educational information** about laws and regulations
- Helping you **understand** government processes
- Assisting with **paperwork preparation** (without legal advice)
- **Directing you** to appropriate professional resources
- **Accompanying you** to appointments (as permitted)
- Helping you **organize** your documents and questions

### What We CAN Help With:
- Explaining publicly available information
- Helping you prepare questions for attorneys/professionals
- Directing you to government resources
- Explaining general business processes
- Providing compliance checklists
- Organizing your documentation

### What We CANNOT Do:
- Provide legal advice
- Represent you in court
- Draft legal documents
- Interpret laws for your specific situation
- Make decisions on your behalf
- Practice law in any form

## Starting a Business - FAQ

### Q: What do I need to start a business in Fresno?

**A:** Generally, you'll need to consider:

1. **Business Structure** - Decide on LLC, Corporation, Partnership, or Sole Proprietorship
   - *Consult an attorney or CPA for advice on which structure is right for you*

2. **Registration** - File with California Secretary of State (for LLCs/Corporations)
   - Website: https://www.sos.ca.gov/business-programs

3. **Business License** - Obtain from City of Fresno
   - Website: https://www.fresno.gov/finance/business-license/
   - Phone: (559) 621-6880

4. **Fictitious Business Name (DBA)** - If operating under a different name
   - File with Fresno County Clerk

5. **EIN (Employer Identification Number)** - From the IRS
   - Free at: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online

6. **Seller's Permit** - If selling tangible goods
   - CDTFA: https://www.cdtfa.ca.gov

**Resource:** SBA Business Guide: https://www.sba.gov/business-guide/launch-your-business/choose-business-structure

---

### Q: How much does it cost to start a business?

**A:** Common startup costs include:

| Item | Approximate Cost |
|------|------------------|
| LLC Filing (CA) | $70 |
| Corporation Filing (CA) | $100 |
| Fresno Business License | Varies (by gross receipts) |
| Fictitious Business Name | ~$50-100 |
| EIN | Free |
| Seller's Permit | Free |
| CA Minimum Franchise Tax | $800/year (most entities) |

*Costs change - verify current fees with agencies*

---

### Q: Do I need a business license if I work from home?

**A:** In most cases, **yes**. The City of Fresno requires business licenses for home-based businesses operating within city limits. Home-based businesses may have:
- Lower fee tiers
- Zoning restrictions
- Limitations on customers visiting
- Restrictions on signage and employees

**Contact:** City of Fresno Finance Department (559) 621-6880

## Employment Questions

### Q: What is the minimum wage in Fresno?

**A:** California sets the statewide minimum wage:
- **2024:** $16.00/hour for all employers
- **Check for annual increases:** Minimum wage typically increases January 1

Some industries have higher minimums (fast food, healthcare). Always verify current rates.

**Resource:** https://www.dir.ca.gov/dlse/faq_minimumwage.htm

---

### Q: What breaks do I have to give employees?

**A:** California requires:

**Meal Breaks:**
- 30-minute unpaid meal break before the 5th hour of work
- Second 30-minute meal break before the 10th hour (if working 10+ hours)

**Rest Breaks:**
- 10-minute paid rest break per 4 hours worked
- Should be in the middle of each work period when practical

**Penalty:** If breaks are not provided, you owe 1 hour of pay per missed break.

**Resource:** https://www.dir.ca.gov/dlse/FAQ_MealPeriods.htm

---

### Q: Do I need workers' compensation insurance?

**A:** **Yes**. California requires workers' compensation insurance for **all employers with employees**, even if you have just one part-time employee.

**Penalties for non-compliance:**
- Criminal misdemeanor
- Fines up to $100,000
- Stop-work order

**Where to get it:**
- Private insurance carriers
- State Compensation Insurance Fund: https://www.statefundca.com

---

### Q: How do I pay my employees correctly?

**A:** California requirements include:

1. **Pay at least minimum wage** (currently $16.00/hr)
2. **Overtime:**
   - 1.5x after 8 hours/day
   - 2x after 12 hours/day
   - 1.5x for first 8 hours on 7th consecutive day
3. **Itemized pay stubs** with required information
4. **Timely payment:**
   - Bi-weekly or semi-monthly (most common)
   - Final wages: Immediately if fired, within 72 hours if quit
5. **Record keeping** - Maintain payroll records for 3+ years

**Resource:** https://www.dir.ca.gov/dlse/faq_paydays.htm

---

### Q: What posters do I need to display?

**A:** Required state and federal posters include:
- California Minimum Wage
- Payday Notice
- Cal/OSHA Safety
- Workers' Compensation
- Discrimination/Harassment (5+ employees)
- Federal FLSA
- Federal OSHA
- EEO (15+ employees)

**Free posters:**
- State: https://www.dir.ca.gov/wpnodb.html
- Federal: https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/posters

## Tax Questions

### Q: What taxes does my business need to pay?

**A:** Depending on your business, you may owe:

**Federal:**
- Federal income tax
- Self-employment tax (if applicable)
- Payroll taxes (if you have employees)
- FUTA (unemployment)

**California:**
- State income tax (FTB)
- LLC fee ($800 minimum + gross receipts fee for LLCs)
- Sales tax (if selling tangible goods)
- Payroll taxes (EDD): UI, ETT, SDI withholding

**Local:**
- Business license tax
- Possibly other local fees

*Consult a CPA for your specific tax obligations*

---

### Q: When are taxes due?

**A:** Key deadlines:

| Tax | Due Date |
|-----|----------|
| Federal Estimated Tax | Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15 |
| CA Estimated Tax | Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15 |
| Federal/State Annual Return | Varies by entity (Mar 15 or Apr 15 typically) |
| Payroll Taxes | Per deposit schedule (monthly or semi-weekly) |
| Sales Tax | Monthly, quarterly, or annually based on volume |

---

### Q: Do I need to collect sales tax?

**A:** Generally, **yes** if you sell tangible personal property (physical goods) in California.

**Exempt items include:**
- Most food for home consumption
- Prescription medicine
- Sales for resale (with valid resale certificate)

**Sales tax rate in Fresno:** Varies by exact location (state + local)
- Use CDTFA rate lookup: https://www.cdtfa.ca.gov/taxes-and-fees/rates.aspx

**Get a Seller's Permit:** https://www.cdtfa.ca.gov

### Q: What licenses and permits do I need?

**A:** Common requirements:

| If You... | You May Need |
|-----------|--------------|
| Operate any business | City business license |

**Check:**

- Can be renewed online, by mail, or in person
- Have penalties for late renewal

**Contact:**

- Phone: (559) 621-6880

## Common Business Challenges

### Q: Someone owes me money. What can I do?

**A:** Options may include:

1. **Direct communication** - Request payment in writing
2. **Payment plan** - Negotiate terms
3. **Collection agency** - Third-party collection
4. **Small claims court** - For amounts up to $10,000 (businesses)
5. **Civil lawsuit** - For larger amounts (consult an attorney)

**Small Claims Resource:** https://www.courts.ca.gov/selfhelp-smallclaims.htm

**Fresno County Small Claims:** (559) 457-1900

*For significant amounts, consult an attorney*

---

### Q: I received a government notice. What should I do?

**A:**

1. **Read carefully** - Understand what's being requested
2. **Note deadlines** - Many notices have response deadlines
3. **Don't ignore it** - Ignoring often makes things worse
4. **Gather documents** - Collect relevant records
5. **Respond timely** - Meet all deadlines
6. **Get help if needed** - Complex issues may require professional assistance

**For legal notices or lawsuits:** Consult an attorney immediately

---

### Q: How do I handle a customer complaint?

**A:** General guidance:

1. **Listen** - Let them explain the issue
2. **Acknowledge** - Show you understand their concern
3. **Document** - Keep records of complaints
4. **Resolve** - Offer a fair solution
5. **Follow up** - Ensure satisfaction
6. **Learn** - Use feedback to improve

**For legal threats:** Consult an attorney

---

### Q: What insurance do I need?

**A:** Common business insurance includes:

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| General Liability | Third-party injuries, property damage |
| Professional Liability | Errors and omissions |
| Workers' Compensation | Employee injuries (REQUIRED) |
| Commercial Property | Business property protection |
| Commercial Auto | Business vehicle coverage |
| Business Interruption | Lost income from covered events |

*Consult an insurance broker for your specific needs*
